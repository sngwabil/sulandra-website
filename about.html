What We Do

Sulandra provides home and community-based supports that are practical, consistent, and aligned to the person’s goals. We build services around real life—not paperwork—so people can experience stability, growth, and community belonging.

Our service model: stable, person-centered, measurable

People thrive when support is predictable and respectful. Our teams work with individuals, families/guardians, county boards, and interdisciplinary partners to create service plans that fit the person’s daily environment and build skills over time.

Foundation

Safety and dignity

Support is delivered with professionalism, boundaries, and respect—protecting the person’s rights while reducing risk. We treat safety as a daily practice, not a policy on a shelf.

Execution

Consistency and follow-through

We prioritize reliable staffing, clear documentation, and active supervision so plans become real routines. Families know what to expect—and who to call.

What we provide (core supports)

Community Living Supports Respite &s Skill Building & Independence Community Inclusion Care Coordination Support Medication Support (non-medical) Behavioral Support Coordination Transportation Support (as authorized)

How we work (from intake to ongoing support)

Phase What you can expect How we keep it strong
1) Welcome & Discovery We listen first—what’s working, what’s hard, what matters most. We review authorizations, risks, routines, strengths, and supports already in place. Clear intake checklist, risk screening, communication plan, emergency contacts, and service expectations.
2) Person-Centered Plan Goals become practical routines: skill steps, community participation, health/safety supports, and family/guardian preferences. Documentation standards, training plan for staff, measurable outcomes, and supervisor review.
3) Staffing & Training Thoughtful matching, orientation, and competency checks—so the right staff are prepared for the person’s needs. Background checks, role-based training, coaching, and early shift shadowing before independent assignment.
4) Support in Real Life Support shows up on time, follows the plan, documents correctly, and communicates changes quickly. Supervisory visits, shift notes review, outcome tracking, and continuous feedback loops.
5) Improve & Prevent We learn from feedback and incidents, strengthen plans, and raise the quality bar over time. Incident review, corrective action, refresher training, audits, and ongoing improvement.
Our promise to families, guardians, and partners
  • Clear communication: You will know who is supporting, who is supervising, and how to reach us.
  • Professionalism: Staff are trained for boundaries, dignity, confidentiality, and respectful support.
  • Reliability: We plan for coverage, staffing transitions, and continuity.
  • Transparency: Concerns are logged, addressed, and followed through.
  • Outcome focus: Services are evaluated by real improvements in daily life and stability.
